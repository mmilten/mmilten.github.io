Central Processing Unit

The central processing unit (CPU) of a computer is the core component of a computer, it's the brains of the computer. This is a chip that sits in a socket in the motherboard. The CPU takes instructions from a program or application and performs calculations. CPUs have a number of cores and clock speed that determines the performances of its calculations. All modern CPUs today are atleast dual-core, while cores can go to quad-core, six, eight, 10, 12, and 16-core CPUs. Right now there are two popular brands of CPUs on the market the Intel and AMD line of chipsets.

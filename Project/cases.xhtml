Cases

The first thing you will need to build a computer is the case in which all your other parts are going to be in. There is a variety of sizes and styles of cases one can choose from. One of the biggest consideration when choosing between the different sizes of cases is the number of slots and number of devices one would like to have. But the most important factor is the compatiblity of a case with motherboard size.

Sizes

  1. Full Tower
    1. The biggest of the cases where you can install big GPU like the new nVidia GeForce RTX 3090. With the amoung of space you can use a water-cooling solution for you cooling needs for your system.
  2. Mid Tower
    1. A step down from the full tower case, the mid-tower case are the most widely used computer cases. Mid-towers can contain 2 to 4 internal drive bays and external drive bays. This case can support an all in one (AIO) cooling setup for the PC.
  3. Micro PC Tower
    1. These are the smaller than the mid-tower case where only 2 to 3 internal drive bays are supported. These cases usually have a problem later in the future due to expandability issues.
